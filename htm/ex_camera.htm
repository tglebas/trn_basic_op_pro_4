Exercise: Camera Control

Objective

This exercise helps participants learn how to control the camera to aid in piloting and to observe underwater objects.

Preparation

  • Select an above water landmark that can be used as a reference for the navigation task.
  • Select a submerged target that can be observed up close.

Technique

Use the camera tilt indicator to set the camera position.

Surface the ROV and tilt the camera up so that you can see out of the water. Turn the ROV to locate your landmark and navigate in the direction of the landmark.

Tilt the camera all the way up or down and try to navigate in a straight line without using the compass as a reference.

Move close to a submerged object and adust the focus for a clear view.

Tips

  • The sun makes a good navigational reference.
  • Don't tilt the camera all the way up when trying to navigate on the surface. Adjust the camera tilt for the best view of your landmark.
  • When navigating on the surface, don't use too much vertical thrust up. This may create bubbles that can obscure your view.
