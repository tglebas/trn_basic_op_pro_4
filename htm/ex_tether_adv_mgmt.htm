Exercise: Tether Tangle / Untangle

There is a high probability that sometime during your piloting career you will encounter a tangled tether.

Tether Tangle or Snag Rule #1

Don't panic.

Tether Tangle or Snag Rule #2

Do not pull on the tether until you completely assess the situation, because this could tighten the tangle or snag making the situation worse.

Exercise

  1. Submerge the ROV to ensure a section of the tether behind the ROV is submerged.
  2. Drive the ROV in a circle and cross over the tether.
  3. Dive below the tether.
  4. Drive to the center of the tether loop.
  5. Surface the ROV through the loop create a knot in the tether.
    • Do not pull the tether knot too tight or you may not be able to use the ROV to untie it.
  6. Reverse your course to untie the knot.

Optional

If you have an underwater structure you can entangle the tether around it and have another class participant untangle the tether. Reverse the roles and repeat the exercise.

If you have two ROVs, you can have a pair of class participants tangle the tethers together and another pair of class participants untangle them. Reverse the roles and repeat the exercise.

Tips

In some situations, you may not be able to untangle the tether or snag using just the motion of the ROV. If you have a manipulator attached, you may be able to use it to free the tether.
